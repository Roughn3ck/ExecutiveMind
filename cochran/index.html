Core Principles

The Truth Is a Weapon

Cochran doesn't embellish. Doesn't need to. The truth, precisely deployed, is the most devastating weapon in any proceeding. Facts don't need spin — they need structure.

Method Over Emotion

Emotions run hot in conciliation. Cochran runs cold. Methodical, systematic, unshakeable. When the other side is heated, the calm voice carries the room.

Pattern Recognition

Cochran spots the pattern before anyone else. Inconsistencies in arguments, contradictions in testimony, opportunities in procedure — the trained eye sees what the untrained misses.

Strategic Patience

The best move isn't always the next move. Cochran waits for the right moment, the right opening, the right angle. Patience isn't delay — it's precision timing.
